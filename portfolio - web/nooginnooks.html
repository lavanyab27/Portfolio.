01 ¬∑ Overview

Why Noogin Nooks?

Students are often given one-size-fits-all content. Noogin Nooks explores what happens when the format of learning is as adaptive as the topics themselves.

Problem statement ¬∑ How might we increase student engagement and retention by delivering curriculum-aligned micro-lessons in formats that match each student's learning style?

  • Generic learning content rarely considers how different students absorb information. Time is spent, but little sticks.
  • During onboarding, students take a short quiz that detects their dominant learning preferences‚Äîvisual, auditory, linguistic, or kinesthetic‚Äîalong with confidence in each subject.
  • Lessons are delivered as 5‚Äì10 minute micro-sessions as well as long lessons: visuals & diagrams for visual learners, concise audio recaps for auditory learners, text + examples for linguistic learners, and quick hands-on prompts for kinesthetic learners.
  • An AI mascot, Nook, guides each learner with friendly feedback, nudges, and suggestions to try alternate formats when attention drops.
Users
Students in classes 6‚Äì10, parents who want visibility into learning, and tutors who need a tool that complements their sessions.
Solution
An onboarding quiz + adaptive content engine that assembles each lesson in the right format, plus a dashboard that surfaces progress and confidence across topics.
Constraints
Mobile-first, low cognitive load, minimal onboarding friction, and modular content that can be reused across boards and curriculums.

02 ¬∑ Research

Understanding the space

I combined competitor analysis, stakeholder mapping, and user interviews to understand where current ed-tech tools succeed‚Äîand where they fail students and parents.

Affinity themes

Need a single hub

Students want one place where school topics, practice and recap material live together, instead of jumping across apps.

Motivation & FOMO

They don't want to miss "important" concepts or exams. Visible streaks and progress give a sense of momentum.

Parents want proof

Parents care less about fancy visuals and more about clear progress, time spent, and how to support at home.

Research themes & problems

Learning behaviour
  • Short, structured sessions increase completion rates.
  • Format mismatch causes low retention even when time is spent.
Parental needs
  • Parents want simple progress metrics and suggestions for support at home.
  • They need reassurance that screen time is actually productive.
Content gap
  • Platforms optimise for content breadth, not for how it is delivered.
  • Opportunity: personalised micro-learning assembled from modular content blocks.

Key insights

  • Short wins matter: Students respond better to 5‚Äì10 minute focused lessons than long lectures.
  • Format matters: Delivering the same concept in different formats significantly improves recall.
  • Parental trust: Simple dashboards and suggested home activities make parents more confident about the app.

Meet Nook ‚Äì The learning companion

Nook is the friendly AI mascot that guides students through their learning journey. Designed to feel approachable and encouraging, Nook adapts its tone based on student progress and provides contextual nudges when needed.

  • Personality: Friendly, patient, and subtly motivating without being pushy or infantilizing.
  • Role: Suggests next steps, celebrates wins, and gently encourages trying alternate formats when a concept isn't clicking.
  • Visual design: Soft, rounded character with warm gradient colors that match the app's accent palette.
  • Tone: Conversational and age-appropriate, adapting language for different grade levels.
